Honours Research Focus: Unsupervised Anomaly Detection in FinTech

Proposed Postgraduate Research | Target: BSc Honours in Computer Science and Information Sciences

As I transition into postgraduate studies, my primary academic goal is to address high-stakes challenges in financial technology and cybersecurity. I am proposing a comparative study to evaluate resource-efficient, unsupervised anomaly detection models against skewed credit card transaction data.

Proposed Research Topic:

"A Comparative Evaluation of Unsupervised Anomaly Detection Algorithms for Credit Card Fraud Detection"

Research Highlights:

  • Algorithm Comparison: Implementing and evaluating unsupervised models (like Isolation Forest) against optimized baseline classifiers (such as Support Vector Machines) to evaluate their performance on highly imbalanced datasets.
  • Data Pipeline Engineering: Architecting an optimized pipeline to handle extreme data skewness using robust scaling, Principal Component Analysis (PCA), and synthetic oversampling methods (SMOTE).
  • FinTech Performance Metrics: Measuring architectures strictly on Classification Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-score to identify structures that minimize false positives and maintain low operational latency.
